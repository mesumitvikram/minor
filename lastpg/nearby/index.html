FLORA AND FAUNA

Flora and Fauna

The Nalanda district of Bihar is home to a variety of flora and fauna. The Rajgir Wildlife Sanctuary in the Nalanda district is home to:
Mammals: Elephants, panthers, leopards, lesser cats, wild dogs, barking deer, and pigmy dogs.
Reptiles: Indian cobras, pythons, monitor lizards, and hilly lizards.
Birds: Blue bull, chital or spotted deer, Indian crested porcupine, small Indian civet, jungle cat, painted spurfowl, Eurasian thick knee, and painted sandgrouse are generally found in these regions.
The Rajgir Wildlife Sanctuary also has 28 species of larger mammals, over 183 species of birds, 39 species of reptiles, 11 species of amphibians, 13 species of fishes, 51 species of butterflies, and 6 species of scorpions. The Nalanda forest division has 148 species of plants, including 52 dicotyledons families and 4 monocotyledons families. The Nalanda district also has the Pant Wildlife Sanctuary, which is located near Rajgir and covers 13.8 square miles.
