About Nalanda

Nalanda holds immense historical significance as an ancient center of learning and intellectual pursuit in India. Established around the 5th century CE, it became one of the world's first residential universities, attracting scholars from various parts of Asia. Its curriculum covered a wide range of subjects, including philosophy, astronomy, medicine, and mathematics. Nalanda played a pivotal role in the transmission of knowledge, fostering an environment that encouraged debate and critical thinking. The renowned scholars like Aryabhata, Nagarjuna, and Bodhidharma are believed to have been associated with Nalanda. The library of Nalanda was a treasure trove of ancient manuscripts, making it a center for the preservation and dissemination of knowledge. Unfortunately, the university faced destruction during invasions, notably in the 12th century by Turkic invaders led by Bakhtiyar Khilji. Despite its physical decline, Nalanda's legacy endured, influencing educational traditions across Asia and contributing to the rich tapestry of India's intellectual history. The ruins of Nalanda stand as a poignant reminder of the scholarly excellence that once thrived within its walls, symbolizing the enduring quest for knowledge in human history.
